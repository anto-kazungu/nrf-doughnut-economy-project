The Project: Living Within the Doughnut

Reimagining Thrive: Social, Ecological, and Economic

This project, led by the Youth for Ngong Road Forest (YNRF), explores how urban forests can model a just and regenerative future. Our mission is to move beyond simple economic growth and reimagine what it means for a community to thrive economically, ecologically, and socially at the same time. The core philosophy guiding our efforts is Kate Raworth's Doughnut Economics Model.

The Doughnut Explained

The Doughnut defines a safe and just space for humanity. We strive to meet the Social Foundation (ensuring no one falls into shortfall for basic needs like food, health, and water) while remaining within the Ecological Ceiling (avoiding overshoot on planetary boundaries like climate change and biodiversity loss). Ngong Road Forest serves as our local laboratory for this balance.

Local Application: Economies of Care

Inspired by principles of community governance (like the Garvagh Principle), we are implementing practical strategies to embed care and collaboration into the forest's management:

  • 1.

    Participatory Budgeting & Shared Funds: Piloting a youth-led micro-grant system or shared fund to empower the community to directly decide how resources are allocated for forest activities and eco-innovation.

  • 2.

    Commoning: Reinforcing the fundamental idea that the forest is a shared commons —a space that belongs to residents, youth, nature, and is welcoming to everyone.

  • 3.

    Reflective Gatherings: Hosting regular "Doughnut Circles" after major cleanups or planting events to facilitate community dialogues, reflect on our progress, and collaboratively plan future actions.
